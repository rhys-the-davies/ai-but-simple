# AI starter pack for small businesses

**By Studio Rhys**

---

## What this is

Most small businesses are already using AI tools like Claude or ChatGPT to draft emails, summarise documents, and answer questions. What they're not doing is connecting those tools to their own systems, their own data, and their own way of doing things.

This pack changes that.

It gives you what you need to connect your internal tools to an AI assistant, and to teach that assistant how your organisation communicates and operates. The result: responses that draw on your live data and sound like your team wrote them - not generic AI output.

This is not a technical product. You do not need to be a developer to use it. Some parts will require a short conversation with whoever built or manages your platform - this pack tells you exactly what to say to them.

If someone has ever sent you a Skill or an AI template and you didn't know what to do with it - this is where you start.

---

## What you'll be able to do

Once you've worked through this pack, you'll be able to:

- Ask your AI assistant questions about your own business and get real, live answers
- Connect the tools your team already uses - Airtable, Google Workspace, Notion, and others - to your AI assistant
- Generate emails, reports, and descriptions that match your organisation's voice
- Brief a developer clearly if you need a custom connection built for your own platform

---

## What's in this pack

**1. Context** - a short guide explaining what MCPs and Skills are, why they matter, and how they work together. Read this first. There's also an audio version if you'd rather listen.

**2. Discovery questionnaire** - structured questions about your organisation, your users, and how you communicate. Fill this in before anything else. It becomes the raw material for your Skills.

**3. MCP connection guide** - a directory of tools that already have MCP connections available, with step-by-step instructions for the most common ones. If your tool is on this list, you can connect it yourself today.

**4. Developer brief template** - a fill-in document you hand to a developer if you need a custom MCP connection built for your own platform.

**5. Skills template** - an annotated template explaining what a good Skill looks like and how to write one. Includes examples comparing generic AI output with Skills-informed output.

**6. Starter Skills library** - pre-built Skills covering the most common use cases: customer emails, internal reports, product or space descriptions, and onboarding communications. Adapt these using your discovery questionnaire answers.

**7. Setup instructions** - a single-page guide for putting it all together, written for non-technical users.

## Prerequisites

**Claude Pro.** Go to claude.ai, create an account, and upgrade to Pro. It costs €18/month. This pack is built and tested for Claude Pro - everything in it assumes that's what you're using. If you want to adapt any part of this pack for ChatGPT, Gemini, or a free Claude account, contributions are welcome on GitHub.

**A list of your tools.** Make a note of the digital tools your team uses day-to-day - your CRM, project management tool, communication platform, and your own product if you have one. You'll refer to this throughout.

**Access to whoever manages your platform.** If your organisation has a custom-built tool or platform, you'll need to be able to have a short conversation with the developer or agency that built it. This pack tells you exactly what to ask them.

**Two to four hours.** The first pass - reading the context, completing the questionnaire, connecting your first tool, and adapting your first Skill - takes most people between two and four hours.

---

## A note on what this won't do

This pack gets you to a working setup. The quality of what you get out depends on the quality of what you put in, particularly in your Skills. The starter Skills are a first draft, not a finished product.

It also won't replace the judgment of your team. AI connected to your systems is a tool for moving faster and asking better questions. The decisions still belong to you.

---

## How this pack relates to Anthropic's Skills ecosystem

Anthropic - the company that makes Claude - maintains an official Skills repository at github.com/anthropics/skills. It's well-built and growing fast. It's also written for developers.

This pack is the non-technical layer on top of it. We pull Skills from Anthropic's repo, write plain-English guides for them, and make them accessible to people who have no interest in markdown files or GitHub. When the community here builds something new, we contribute it back upstream so the whole ecosystem benefits.

The goal is not to compete with or replace what Anthropic is building. It's to make sure the people who need it most can actually use it.
